Manifesto
Software should respect the people who use it.

Attention is not a resource to harvest.

The best software helps you do something meaningful, then gets out of the way. It does not need streaks, traps, or artificial urgency to prove its value.

Privacy is not a feature toggle.

It should shape the product from the first line of code: what gets stored, what never leaves your device, and what the software can do without asking for more.

Ownership still matters.

Tools should feel like they belong to the person using them. Installable, portable, offline-capable software is not nostalgia. It is respect.

Small can be a discipline.

Ogbara Labs is deliberately narrow. Fewer products. More care. Slower decisions. Better maintenance. Less noise between the maker and the person using the work.

Studio principles

The standard behind the software.

These are not taglines. They are constraints that shape product decisions.

01

Respect attention

No engagement traps. No artificial urgency. The product should justify the time it asks for.

02

Privacy by default

Data minimisation comes first. Tracking is not the price of using the work.

03

Own your data

Where possible, your information stays with you. Sync should be optional, understandable, and respectful.

04

Offline first

Good software should keep working when the connection does not.

05

Built to last

Products are maintained like long-term tools, not campaigns that expire after launch.

06

Independent by choice

Staying small protects the work from incentives that would make it worse.

The studio

Small enough to care about every decision.

Ogbara Labs is an independent studio in Melbourne, built around the belief that software still benefits from a visible maker, a point of view, and a long memory.

There is no growth-at-all-costs roadmap here. No ad network to satisfy. No investor deck asking users to become metrics. Just a small body of work, improved with care.

The advantage of staying small is accountability. Every detail has somewhere to land. Every product carries the studio's name. Every decision has to be lived with.

Build the thing properly. Keep it honest. Improve it long after the launch moment has passed.
— Ogbara Labs
